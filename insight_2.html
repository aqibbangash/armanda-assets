Why do we love Uncertainty?

Published on May 3, 2019

Human Nature. That’s it..

Anthropologically speaking, our software is designed to hunt, gather and bring it home. Whether it’s lucky number 7 at the roulette table or ticking the right box at the polling station, we love the thrill. The thrill of the hunt, the improbability that pumps adrenaline at the Tuesday night lotto number draws. We can’t help it. We need to speculate our positive chances, to drive us to our goal or at least get close to it.

There is an art to speculation which is unique to everyone. We buy that lotto ticket and hope for the best, speculating our winning chances while sipping on a golden mojito on our private yacht off the coast of imagination land. There needs to be a line where speculation ends and daydreaming begins as it can cost you.

“There are two times in a man’s life when he should not speculate: when he can’t afford it, and when he can.”- Mark Twain

This update aims to address the dreamers. Dreams do come true, but only if you are not in them. Let’s look at the markets where we left them last time.

The markets have been in a cradle the last 30 days with some potentially positive news coming out, where some pundits say the Australia property market is seeing a slight recovery. By recovery they mean it is less negative. Our definition of recovery does not mean a minus 11% YOY growth in the best performing city, it is still alarming.

Loans to Real-estate investors are currently falling at the highest pace since in the past 3 years. This means that the property market will recover but at a snail’s pace. JP Morgan rang some alarm bells by pointing to 2020 as the year of Armageddon. They also pointed out that the 2008 pattern may repeat itself, we say those who don’t learn from history might actually repeat it.

If you haven’t been living under a rock, you know about the infinity stones and the whole Disney Marvel saga surrounding it. As shown in those movies the stones were bad for business, we saw these 6 suburbs bad for business with the highest negative growth recorded in 2018:

  • Beachmere QLD 4510 (-49.8 percent)
  • Mangerton NSW 2500 (-43.9 percent)
  • Gladstone Central QLD 4680 (-42.1 percent)
  • Berserker QLD 4701 (-38.9 percent)
  • Aberfeldie VIC 3040 (-35.8 percent)
  • Springwood QLD 4127 (-35.6 percent)

Yeah, we were shocked too. Amid all this property market turmoil the biggest losers have been off-the-plan apartment investors. An interesting stat surrounding it is the 2 out of every 3 sold haven’t made money in a span of past 8 years in a city such as Melbourne. In Brisbane people have been getting rid of these for a loss.

Positive news came from the Europe parliament which has banned single-use plastic items. We have already positioned ourselves with Chatstone's parent company VMI (Vending machine International) who have signed deals or are in negotiations with some of the largest beverages companies in the world.
