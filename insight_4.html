Keep your eye on the ball

Published on July 15, 2019

The New Year brings in new freedoms and new challenges. The financial year 2018-2019 showed promise but midway lost that momentum. Elections, economic recession, and the infamous property crisis have brewed uncertainty into the mix. Passengers onboard the property train may not know where to get off and investors on the platform are afraid to jump on.

These analogies keep on coming, but some encouragement from the RBA is yet to be seen. In the last article, there was an insight into the rate cut from RBA and the way it will affect savings. This article is now coming off as a supplement for the former. The Reserve Bank has cut interest rates to a historic low of 1 percent. If you are not aware of this, this is the second time in 30 days.

Keeping your eye on the ball may be tough in full swing, but a few pro tips can make it easy.

  • If you are not making a considerable return above the inflation rate you are losing money. The current rate of inflation in Australia is 1.90%. If the term deposit rate is 2.50% pa your profits will be (2.50 -1.90) 0.6%.
  • Each and every investment carries risk. The conventional principle is high risk carries high rewards and vice versa. However conventional principles do not apply to contemporary investments. Contemporary investment products may not be pegged to the financial markets reducing their risks.
  • Spread it out. Don’t plan on hitting the ball on every opportunity. Diversify your portfolio and keep a certain percentage of diverse investments in there. Diversification stabilizes returns and the risk profile is kept to a minimum.
  • Do not trust your financial advisors blindly. Probably the best pro tip of all, no one will care about your money as much you and you should be the one making decisions. You might not get the best financial advisor and the fee might reduce your total returns. Keep your head in the game.
  • Remove fees. If there are processes that are charging you extra fees for frivolous reasons, eliminate them immediately. Change your service or bank. Go for the low fee option. Fees are an integral part of return calculation and must be minimized in order to maximize your earnings.
  • Set it and forget it. Investments should not be a headache, only brokers and investment bankers need to look at the market numbers every 30 seconds. You have better things to do with your time. Look for passive investments. Make your money work for you not the other way around.

It has been one of those good and bad start to the new financial year. The RBA starts it with a bang, introducing a second 0.25% interest rate cut creating history. The previous rate cut was able to bring the Sydney property market back up but only by a mere 1%. JP Morgan expects that there could be another 2 rate cuts by Christmas 2019. A sigh of relief which was promised before the elections are now being delivered in small doses. For the new property investor, the current rate cut might seem like a point of relief, but seasoned investors are sitting in the corner waiting. It seems as though rock bottom is still a bit further away and could possibly come by the mid to end of 2020.
