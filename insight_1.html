Make way for Alternatives

Published on April 10, 2019

Unfortunately, the weather in today's market makes you wish you had kept that umbrella with you. Let's be grateful for alternatives and this update to bring you up to speed on the new normal in investment strategy.

In this update, I want to give you insight into the markets as seen by our investment managers and seasoned investors. But wait, what is an alternative investment? Don't worry I have that sorted for you.

I love scary stories. Like the Australian property market which was seen as safe heaven not less than a few years ago is now coming down like a house of cards. Investors running scared by Labor's announcement on negative gearing changes claiming that fun times are now over. Property values have now fallen 13.9 percent in Sydney since their peak in July 2017 and experts warn prices could remain stuck in a “deep trough”. In Melbourne, apartment prices are expected to decrease 5 percent this year and another 1.4 percent in 2020. This compares with the annual fall of 4.6 percent in 2009 during the GFC.

Across the nation overall, Moody’s expects house prices in major cities to fall 7.7 percent this year, while apartments will see a smaller 4.3 percent decline, according to the rating agency report. The biggest fall in house prices is expected to remain in the Ryde area, in the city’s northwest, with a 15.8 percent fall tipped for this year.

Let's talk about the one where Bloomberg tells us on how the Australian government 10-year note fell 6 basis points making this a New Record Low. Even the big boys of liberty are feeling the heat with U.S Treasury yield in a downward curve for the first time since 2007.

Translation: Property is NOT SAFE to invest anymore. Government bonds may not be as profitable.

In these times of uncertainty and low returns, seasoned investors look for alternative options or simply put, they never forget their umbrella at home. Diversification is key. If there's any indication of a market risk-free investment, do consider it. If you like to play roulette with your savings, please, by all means, buy a house.

Australian companies with ethical investment opportunities and fixed returns with minimum market risk are often ignored as they do not have high budget marketing campaigns or the glamour of a cocaine-fueled wall street stock broker. While the masses bet on the stock market, ethical investors bet on firms which have created electric cars, plastic dissolving ocean spheres and water treatment plants where they need it the most. I urge investors to reconsider their investment strategies and make space for solid ethical investments in their portfolios while also earning a great return.
